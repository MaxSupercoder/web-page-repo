Why did Mesopotamia Flood?

Every year the Tigris and Euphrates rivers would flood. The Tigris and Euphrates rivers both originate from high mountains, high enough to be in the clouds where it would rain, the water/rain would run down the mountains, steep slopes(which gave the rivers their water flow) and into the rivers. In winter, the water would freeze on the mountain tops, then in summer the ice(that has been increasing in size all through winter) would all melt into water which would run down the mountain, into the rivers, and because the ice has been increasing to mass amounts during winter all that water would cause the rivers to overflow and flood.



How Did The Sediment Get To The soil?

Every year, floods on the Tigris and Euphrates rivers brought silt, a mixture of rich soil and tiny rocks, to the land. The fertile silt made the land ideal for farming. The Tigris and Euphrates rivers derived most of their sediment in the form of snow and rain(probably from the snowy mountains). The rivers then carry the sediment down and deposite it into the soil. Whenever the rivers would flood, the sediment in the rivers would sink onto the soil making it fertile.
